About me

Hi!

My name is Adrien, I'm a French guy who currently lives in Paris, France.

I have loved programming and playing drums since I was 6.

We may have met while I was involved in the following projects: Startup Tour (cf videos), Openwhyd, fhacktory hackathons, or man is not a bird.

What I do as a freelance professional

I develop Node/Meteor/JS web applications and tools. I am also good at evaluating and coaching developers.

I value:

  • Efficiency in the development process;
  • Transparency in my intents, my communication, and my code;
  • Integrity with my interests and convictions.

How I work

  • I currently charge 120€ per hour. I work no more than 5 hours per day, 5 days per week.
  • I am available for missions during most of the summer.
  • I accept flat fee invoicing for very simple projects only (i.e. < €2000). Otherwise, we'll work the Agile way: user stories to decide and prioritize with you, 2-week sprints, per-sprint billing.

Professional portfolio

  • Whyd / Openwhyd(2011-2016)

    I built the full stack of Openwhyd (formerly known as whyd.com), a web-based music curation platform that allows adding tracks using a browser extension (bookmarklet), and plays them in sequence. After open-sourcing the product, I'm now leading its community of contributors.

    Listen to my favorite tracks

    Watch a demo

  • Teaching @ EEMI(2015-2016)

    I teach Javascript programming to three classes of 1st year students, at the Ecole Européenne des Métiers de l'Internet. The first school founded by Xavier Niel (Free), Antoine Granjon (vente-privee.com), and Marc Simoncini (Meetic). I also craft modern pedagogical tools for my students, and for automated evaluation.

    Cours de JavaScript

  • Built Panoply's web app(2016)

    I developed this mobile-first rental store for luxury clothing in 6 sprints of 2 weeks each, with help from Thomas Jeanneau for the front-end.
    #MeteorJS, #Stripe, #Chimp

  • Coaching @ Ayni(2015-2016)

    Ayni is a social learning platform made so that elderly can teach their langage to students of the same professional domain. As technical advisor, I structure the collaboration of the agile development team, and help on the start-up's strategic decisions. I also contributed to their MeteorJS prototype in the frame of Hack4europe.

  • Built hackathon.com(2015)

    Given a spec and a few designs, BeMyApp asked me to develop a responsive-design website to list all hackathons around the world. Besides having to be SEO-enabled, the product should also be able to detect and agregate events and their Twitter updates automatically, using several APIs.
    #nodejs #mongodb #jquery

  • FreelanceRepubliK(2015)

    I setup an agile workflow to improve the performance of the development team, and participated to the development and debugging of a few features on the website, using Node.js, Mongoose and AngularJS.

  • Built Deepki DIT & LCA(2015)

    I built dashboards and data-visualization tools based on CSV data files, and a script to automate the conversion of business-specific PDF tables into CSV files.

Side projects (mostly open-source)

  • 1poll(2015-2016)

  • 1task(2015)

    I'm developing an alternative to Todo-list apps that blends task-management and note-management use cases, assists users in breaking down big tasks into smaller one, and allows infinite levels of hierarchy for organizing sub-tasks.

    Subscribe to my productivity newsletter

  • tCup(2015)

    Crafted during Intel IoT Roadshow, TCup is a Intel Edison server (in Node.js) + iPhone app that helps you improve the quality of your tea consumption, by adapting the infusion process accordingly to the type of tea, and by notifying you when your cup of tea has arrived to your favorite drinking temperature.

  • Discuthing(2015)

    I acted as temporary CTO of this experimental and somewhat artistic messaging start-up, by building a prototype using MeteorJS and validating lean startup hypothesis based on user behavior and retention analysis. You can find my early source code on github.

  • Hsbc Statement Parser(2015)

    As my bank did not allow me to download all my statements in text format, I wrote a NodeJS script that converts PDF statements to CSV files.

    Also checkout pdfreader on npm

  • AlbumKeeper(2014)

  • Playem JS(2013)
