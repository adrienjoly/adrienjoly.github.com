What I’m doing now

(This is a now page, and if you have your own site, you should make one, too.)

I’m home in Montreuil, Paris area, France. It's summer and sunny outside!

After working 4.5 years as the lead software developer of a startup company (Whyd), I have become a freelancer.

Over the last 18 months, I have been spending my time on various things:

  • Teaching Javascript programming to undergraduate students.
  • Programming web sites for my clients. (mostly startups)
  • Programming several side-projects, alone or with friends.
  • Travelling to South America, Thailand, Grece, and around Paris too. With my girlfriend Camille, for leisure and for work.
  • Meeting too many people. Starting to appreciate solitude. Then accepting too many Slack invites again.
  • Blogging about phone calls, sharing advice for "idea" people looking for a tech associate, embarrassing stories of my experience as a freelancer, and self-reflection.
  • Writing and shooting a MOOC in French on how to create a startup, the Lean way. (based on a presentation we gave at NUMA, Paris)
  • Thinking and writing a lot about productivity.
  • Practicing improv, and how to draw.
  • And, more recently, after years of drumming in rock bands, I joined a Jazz band!

I feel that I am ready (and really willing) to build a product, and make a business out of it. Now, I just have to find a skilled extrovert that I can trust, to team up with!

I have been sharing a ton of ideas on a public list and on Pitchcard (great service for getting feedback). If you believe that we have similar interests and complementary skills, please contact me!

If my activities or priorities change, I’ll update this page.
